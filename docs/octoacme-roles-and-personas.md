# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners champion customer outcomes, manage the product backlog, and ensure the development team delivers maximum value. They act as the voice of the customer and bridge between stakeholders and the technical team.

### Responsibilities
- Manage and prioritize the product backlog based on business value
- Define and communicate user stories and acceptance criteria
- Represent customer needs and validate solutions meet requirements
- Make trade-off decisions between scope, time, and resources
- Accept or reject completed work based on acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with stakeholders to gather and refine requirements

### Goals
- Maximize value delivered in each sprint or iteration
- Ensure product features align with customer needs and business objectives
- Maintain a clear, prioritized, and well-groomed backlog
- Foster strong collaboration between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily interaction with development team during standups
- Regular backlog refinement and sprint planning sessions
- Weekly alignment meetings with Product Manager and stakeholders
- User story walkthroughs and acceptance reviews

### Key Collaborations
- **Product Managers**: Align on product vision, strategy, and long-term roadmap
- **Technical Lead**: Discuss technical feasibility and architecture implications
- **Business Analyst**: Refine requirements and validate business logic
- **Developers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and provide feedback on implementations
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on timelines and resource allocation

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads set the technical direction, oversee solution architecture, and mentor the development team. They ensure technical decisions align with best practices and long-term maintainability.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Resolve major technical blockers and make architectural decisions
- Review critical code changes and design proposals
- Mentor developers and promote best practices
- Assess technical feasibility of features and estimates
- Ensure code quality, security, and performance standards

### Goals
- Maintain a scalable and maintainable technical architecture
- Reduce technical debt and prevent architectural issues
- Build team technical capabilities through mentoring
- Ensure alignment between technical implementation and business needs

### Typical Communication
- Participate in sprint planning and architectural discussions
- Technical design documentation and architectural decision records
- Code review feedback and pair programming sessions
- Weekly sync with Product Owner and Project Manager

### Key Collaborations
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance, conduct code reviews, and resolve blockers
- **Product Owner**: Discuss technical trade-offs and feasibility of features
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Define testing strategies and quality standards
- **Project Manager**: Communicate technical risks and resource needs
- **Business Analyst**: Ensure technical solutions align with business requirements

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads develop and execute comprehensive test strategies, manage defect triage, and ensure deliverables meet quality standards. They are responsible for overall product quality and test coverage.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test strategies and comprehensive test plans
- Coordinate test execution across unit, integration, and end-to-end testing
- Manage defect tracking, triage, and resolution priorities
- Report quality metrics and test coverage to stakeholders
- Define and enforce quality gates for releases
- Validate that features meet acceptance criteria and requirements

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, defect-free releases
- Achieve comprehensive test coverage across all critical paths
- Minimize escaped defects to production
- Build a culture of quality ownership across the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily participation in standups to report testing status and blockers
- Test plan reviews and defect triage meetings
- Weekly quality metrics reporting to Project Manager
- Sprint retrospectives to improve quality processes

### Key Collaborations
- **Technical Lead**: Align on testing strategies, automation approaches, and quality standards
- **Developers**: Coordinate on test execution, defect reproduction, and fixes
- **Business Analyst**: Validate test scenarios cover all business requirements
- **Product Owner**: Confirm acceptance criteria are testable and met
- **Project Manager**: Report quality risks and impact on release timelines

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical implementation. They gather, analyze, and document requirements, ensuring solutions deliver business value and meet stakeholder expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document detailed business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and workflow diagrams
- Validate that technical solutions meet business requirements
- Facilitate requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Help define and refine acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business needs across all teams
- Minimize requirement ambiguity and rework
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Validate solutions deliver intended business outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Requirement gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Functional specification documents and user flow diagrams
- Regular syncs with Product Owner and Technical Lead
- Validation sessions to confirm solutions meet requirements

### Key Collaborations
- **Product Owner**: Refine user stories, clarify acceptance criteria, and prioritize backlog items
- **Technical Lead**: Ensure technical solutions align with business requirements
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Define test scenarios based on business requirements
- **Stakeholders**: Gather requirements and validate proposed solutions
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and answer implementation questions

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads manage all internal and external project communications, ensuring stakeholders stay informed and aligned. They craft clear, timely updates and maintain consistent messaging across all channels.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute project communication plans
- Create and distribute project status updates and announcements
- Manage stakeholder communications and expectation setting
- Coordinate messaging for releases and major milestones
- Maintain project documentation and ensure accessibility
- Handle escalations requiring executive or stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders have timely, accurate project information
- Maintain transparency and alignment across the organization
- Minimize surprises and manage expectations proactively
- Foster trust through clear and consistent communication

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status reports and stakeholder newsletters
- Release announcements and milestone communications
- Executive briefings and stakeholder presentations
- Communication templates and guidelines for the team

### Key Collaborations
- **Project Manager**: Align on project status, risks, and key messages
- **Product Manager**: Coordinate product launches and feature announcements
- **Change Manager**: Ensure communication supports adoption and transition activities
- **All team members**: Gather updates and ensure accurate representation of work
- **Stakeholders**: Deliver tailored communications based on audience needs

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers coordinate organizational transitions, prepare teams for new processes or tools, and measure adoption success. They ensure changes are implemented smoothly with minimal disruption.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management strategies and transition plans
- Assess change readiness and identify potential resistance
- Design and deliver training programs for new processes or tools
- Measure adoption rates and effectiveness of changes
- Support teams through transitions and address concerns
- Coordinate with stakeholders to minimize business disruption

### Goals
- Achieve high adoption rates for new processes and tools
- Minimize productivity loss during transitions
- Build organizational change capability and resilience
- Ensure sustained adoption beyond initial implementation

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and readiness reports
- Training materials and adoption guides
- Regular check-ins with affected teams and leaders
- Post-implementation adoption metrics and feedback

### Key Collaborations
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate change activities within project timelines
- **Communications Lead**: Align on messaging and stakeholder communications
- **Technical Lead**: Understand technical changes and their impact on teams
- **All team leads**: Partner on training delivery and adoption support
- **Stakeholders**: Secure buy-in and address organizational concerns
